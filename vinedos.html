Queretaro is a thriving wine region located in central Mexico, known for its lush vineyards and diverse range of grapes. With a rich history of wine making dating back centuries, the vineyards of Queretaro produce some of Mexico's finest and most distinctive wines.

La Redonda

It is one of the most visited vineyards in Querétaro, especially for the events that are held regularly such as the 100 Mexican Wines Festival, the Wine Colors and the Harvest Festival, in addition to guided tours and tastings. La Redonda has more than 38 years of winemaking tradition and over time has managed to create high quality products, which is why its wines are among the most recognized in the country.

Finca Sala Vivé by Freixenet

Freixenet's sparkling wines are a worldwide reference and its Querétaro estate is the first sparkling wine estate in the region. This family company has a long winemaking history. It was founded in 1889 in Barcelona, but it was not until almost 100 years later that it set foot on Mexican soil.

Los Rosales

The company was founded in 1970 and for 40 years was dedicated to supplying grapes to various wine producers. It was not until 2011 that they undertook the adventure of making their own wine, differentiating themselves from others by making it with artisanal care throughout the process. Its emblematic life is Salvador, also called Tintorera.
